Whatsapp mobile

The redesign - UX and UI design

September 2018

As society changes ever so frequently, so must the smart products we use and are dependent on. A redesign of the user interface (UI) for one of the basic apps needed for communication with the aim to provide a dynamic and more fluid approach to the mobile platform of the product.

READ MORE

THE PROBLEM

As a platform for messages, audio and video calls, the user interface and the user experience of the mobile application seem to be stagnant. There has not been any improvement to these aspects of the product (not that this prevents the users from achieving their goal) but excessive stagnation will give room for other competitors in the market to overtake Whatsapp.
As someone said, "change is needed because improvements make your business relevant and remain in demand by consumers."

Current user experience and interface problems of the app

Problem #1
There isn't any indicator that distinctively prompts the user to reply to old chats. This is necessary as the user may have omitted some chats which can result in late replies.

Problem #2
The home screen of the app appears to
be generic, nothing shows or indicates personalization. Your picture or your
name is not displayed anywhere.

Problem #3
Current location of audio and video calling buttons at the top right of the screen have a small touch sensitive area (see red boundary
on image). This small area makes it difficult to make a call with one attempt.

Ideation

I had to empathise with the users especially about not being able to freely customise the app without downloading and additional app to do that. There were few ideas which came to my mind on how to make improvements. I moved on to get user feedback and how these improvements might help the overall usage of the app. When the feedback was positive then I went on to wireframe sketching.
